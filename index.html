Executive Order Imposing Economic Sanctions on Cuban Government Officials and Entities

Original Post

This executive order authorizes the imposition of economic sanctions against individuals and entities within Cuba's government determined to be responsible for internal repression and actions assessed as threatening to United States national security and foreign policy interests. The order is issued under presidential authority granted by the International Emergency Economic Powers Act, the National Emergencies Act, and section 212(f) of the Immigration and Nationality Act of 1952, which provide the President with mechanisms to regulate commerce and restrict entry into the United States during declared national emergencies or when threats to national security are identified.
